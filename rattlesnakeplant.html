Rattlesnake Plant - Goeppertia Insignis

I rate the Rattlesnake Plant as a green thumb-required plant primarily because of the high water and humidity needs. It is best to not let the soil of this plant dry out. When watering it's best to give it a little bit of water frequently. Many find success with keeping this plant in a terrarium because they are great for maintaining high humidity and moisture levels. This plant is native to the tropical regions of America, which makes sense given the high moisture needs. These plants are pet-friendly and tend to grow upright, which makes them great for tabletops. The best way to propagate your Rattlesnake plant is through division.

Plant Care Quick Guide

Care Level Green Thumb
Light Bright, but indirect light
Water Needs High
Humidity High
Toxicity Pet friendly
Propagation Method Division
Growth Type Upright
